What is AI?

The history of Artificial Intelligence (AI) dates back to ancient times when humans first attempted to create mechanical devices that mimicked human behavior. However, the modern era of AI began in the mid-20th century. In 1956, the term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined at the Dartmouth Conference, marking the official birth of the field. Early AI research focused on symbolic AI, which involved creating rules and logic for machines to follow. During the 1950s and 1960s, AI researchers developed expert systems and symbolic reasoning programs. The field made significant progress in areas like game-playing, natural language processing, and problem-solving. However, AI faced challenges and entered a period known as the "AI winter" in the 1970s and 1980s due to limited computational power and unrealistic expectations. The resurgence of AI came in the 21st century, driven by advances in machine learning, deep learning, and neural networks. Breakthroughs in data availability, processing power, and algorithms enabled AI to excel in tasks like image recognition, language translation, and autonomous vehicles. Today, AI is an integral part of our lives, powering virtual assistants, recommendation systems, and innovative applications across various industries, reshaping the way we work and interact with technology
